### **1.2 Why I Wrote This Book**

Teaching business students about databases and information systems requires more than explaining technical concepts ‚Äî it requires telling the story of how information shapes the way organizations think, act, and compete.

Over years of teaching business analytics and management information systems, I noticed a recurring pattern: students could often recall definitions but struggled to connect *why* databases matter to *how* they improve decision-making and performance. I wanted to close that gap.

This book was written to make the abstract concrete. It blends **technical mastery** with **strategic thinking**, showing that database skills are not an end in themselves but tools for understanding business processes, customers, and innovation. The goal is to make data literacy engaging, visual, and contextual ‚Äî to help students see technology as a language for solving real problems.

### **1.11 Key Terms**

| **Term**                                 | **Definition**                                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Information System (IS)**              | A coordinated set of components for collecting, processing, and distributing information to support decision-making and control. |
| **Management Information Systems (MIS)** | Systems designed to help managers organize, evaluate, and manage information effectively.                                        |
| **Database**                             | A structured collection of data stored and accessed electronically.                                                              |
| **Business Intelligence (BI)**           | The set of technologies and strategies used for data analysis in business decision-making.                                       |
| **Digital Transformation**               | The integration of digital technology into all business areas, fundamentally changing operations and value delivery.             |
